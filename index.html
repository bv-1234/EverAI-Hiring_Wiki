Your recruiter will guide you through any role-specific variations.

PriorityTarget time to offer

🔀 Hiring Process

1️⃣ Step 1 — Submission & CV Review

Owner: Agency → HM or TA

All candidates must be submitted via Ashby. When adding a candidate, you must @mention Bárbara in the notes and include the following template:

  • Location / Timezone
  • Visa / Right to Work
  • LinkedIn Profile
  • Portfolio or GitHub
  • Salary Expectations
  • Preferred Payment Currency
  • Contract Type
  • Availability
  • Notice Period
  • Other Active Processes
  • Interview Recording Link
  • Notes / Summary

➡️ Once submitted, HM or TA will review the CV, recording, and notes in Ashby.

2️⃣ Step 2 — Initial Screening

Owner: Agency or TA (role dependent)

ℹ️

The screening path depends on the role. Your TA contact will confirm which applies.

Path A — Recording required
For designated roles, the agency conducts the initial screen and uploads an interview recording link directly to the Ashby profile.

Path B — Internal TA screens
For all other roles, our internal TA team picks up the screening once the CV is approved. No action needed from the agency.

⚠️

Our long-term goal is for the in-house team to conduct all initial screenings. However, we do not currently have the capacity for us currently, which is why we have these two paths. Thanks for bearing with us! 🙏

3️⃣ Step 3 — Hiring Manager Interview

Owner: HM

The first live interview with the role owner. Typically 30 minutes.

The discussion focuses on:

  • Relevant experience
  • Problem-solving approach
  • Ownership and execution ability
  • Alignment with the role scope

4️⃣ Step 4 — Case Study / Technical Assessment

Owner: HM or team member (role dependent)

Used to assess real-world working ability, quality of thinking, and how the candidate communicates and structures their work.

Format:

  1. Candidate receives an assignment with a deadline*
    1. *Software Engineering roles: 2-hour live coding challenge instead
    2. *Typically 1 week, but it can be shorter for some roles
  2. HM reviews the submission
  3. A follow-up debrief may take place

5️⃣ Step 5 — Cultural Fit Interview

Owner: Leadership (typically CMO or CTO)

A senior-level conversation focused on:

  • Alignment with our working style
  • Decision-making and ownership mindset
  • Communication and collaboration

6️⃣ Step 6 — Reference Checks

Owner: HM or TA

Before moving to offer, we request 3–4 professional references — ideally former managers, senior colleagues and/or direct reports who worked closely with the candidate.

7️⃣ Step 7 — Offer

Owner: TA

Once all stages are complete:

  1. Compensation and contract terms are finalized internally
  2. Offer is presented to the candidate
  3. Contract and onboarding are handled through Deel, by our HR team

Before presenting EverAI to a candidate, make sure they understand what the company does. This is not a standard tech company. Candidates who aren't genuinely excited — or who are uncomfortable with the product — will not pass our process.

Please share the Candidate Handbook with every candidate before submission.

A quick reference for how we expect agency partners to operate. Following these keeps the process fast and the relationship strong.

  • Don't ask candidates for their current or past salary — our ranges are already published in the JD, so this is no longer needed and no longer allowed

  • Don't submit speculatively for roles that are paused or not listed as open
  • Don't submit candidates who require visa sponsorship — we do not sponsor
  • Don't submit candidates from blocked countries or currently employed at off-limits companies
  • Don't re-submit a previously rejected candidate without checking with your TA contact first
  • Don't send volume over quality — three weak profiles damage your standing more than one strong one helps
  • Don't promise candidates a specific timeline or outcome — that's for TA to communicate
  • Don't hide or downplay the NSFW nature of the product to get a candidate through the door

How to Prepare Candidates for Interview(s)

ℹ️

This page gives you everything you need to brief candidates before they enter our process. A well-prepared candidate performs better, drops out less, and reflects well on you and us, increasing our chances of success together.

☑️ How to Prepare Candidates Per Stage

ℹ️

This applies to every candidate, at every level, for every role. Numbers and clarity are what separate the strong candidates in our process.

We are a data-driven company, and we expect candidates to speak the same language. Interviewers will probe for specifics — candidates who rely on vague or general answers will not progress.

Prepare every candidate to:

  • Back everything with numbers: Growth percentages, budget sizes, timelines, user numbers, revenue impact. "I improved conversion" is not enough. "I improved conversion from 1.2% to 2.8% over 3 months by testing X" is what we expect.
  • Show their specific individual impact: Not what the team did, but what they personally owned, decided, or built. Interviewers will ask "what was your role specifically?" for every example.
  • Talk about achievements, not just responsibilities: What did they actually accomplish? What changed because of them? What would not have happened if they hadn't been there?
  • Prepare 2–3 strong success stories: With a clear situation, their specific action, and a measurable outcome. These should be ready to use across multiple stages.
  • Be honest about failures too: We value self-awareness and humility. They should also have an example of something that didn't work, what they learned, and what they'd do differently.

➡️ Before the HM Screening Call

Ask the candidate to:

  • Read the Candidate Handbook
  • Explore Candy.ai and YumeAI, as they should understand the product firsthand
  • Be ready to speak clearly to why they want to work in this space specifically
  • Prepare examples of ownership and impact: times they executed without being told what to do, made a decision under pressure, or built something from scratch

➡️ Before the Case Study / Technical Assessment

  • Remind them that quality and structure matter more than speed
  • Encourage them to ask clarifying questions upfront rather than making assumptions
  • For technical roles: they should be prepared to justify decisions they make / made

➡️ Before the Cultural Fit Interview

This is with a founder (typically CMO or CTO). Prepare candidates for:

  • A direct, fast-paced conversation
  • Questions about ownership, decision-making, and how they handle failure
  • Being honest about what they don't know — humility is valued
  • Sharing concrete examples, not hypotheticals

How and Where We Hire Internationally

ℹ️

🌍 Types of Contracts for International Hiring

We use Deel (deel.com) as our global hiring platform, allowing us to engage top talent in over 160 countries without requiring a local legal entity in every market.

Depending on the country, we can engage candidates in one of two ways:

EOR — Employer of Record
Deel employs the candidate on our behalf. We cover employer social security contributions and statutory benefits as required by local law. Best for candidates who want or require local employment status.

B2B — Contractor ➡️ Preferred
The candidate operates as an independent contractor or through their own legal entity. No employer social security cost on our side. Best for candidates who are set up to work this way and where local regulations allow it.

Our preference by default is B2B. Where costs are low or negligible, EOR or B2B are both viable.

Jurisdiction Risk Guide

🎯 Purpose

This document outlines the legal risks that candidates may face depending on their country of residence when working for an adult content platform. We do not refuse to hire from any location — but we believe candidates deserve full transparency about the legal landscape so they can make an informed decision.

The guide covers both traditional and AI-generated adult content, maps open positions to risk levels, and categorizes countries into three risk tiers.

⚠️

Important: This document does not constitute legal advice. It is a risk-flagging tool. Candidates in higher-risk jurisdictions should be encouraged to seek independent local legal counsel before accepting an offer. All parties should share the relevant risk information with candidates during the hiring process.

🪜 Tier Overview

TierRisk LevelCandidate Disclosure RequiredApplies To
🔴 RED High criminal risk. Candidate must be informed that working for an adult content platform may expose them to criminal liability in their jurisdiction. Mandatory — full written risk disclosure before offer. All positions
🟡 YELLOW Partial or evolving risk. Laws may create exposure depending on role type. Enforcement varies. Mandatory — role-specific risk briefing before offer. Primarily content-facing roles
🟢 GREEN Low risk. Adult content is legal with standard regulatory compliance. Standard onboarding information. All positions

🔴 Red Tier Countries — High Risk (Mandatory Disclosure)

These countries have outright bans on pornographic content (production, distribution, and/or possession). The laws typically cover all pornographic material regardless of medium, meaning AI-generated content is treated identically to filmed content. 🤖 AI-Generated Content — Special Considerations

Since the platform produces AI-generated adult content, the following legal distinctions are important.

AI content does NOT change country-level bans

Countries that ban pornography ban all pornographic material regardless of production method. AI-generated content is treated identically to filmed content in ban countries. The "no real people involved" argument provides zero legal protection in Red tier jurisdictions.

Non-consensual deepfakes are rapidly becoming illegal everywhere

The US TAKE IT DOWN Act (May 2025) makes non-consensual AI-generated intimate images a federal felony. The UK Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 criminalizes even creating such images. The EU violence against women directive addresses this at the European level. This applies even in Green tier countries.

AI-generated CSAM is universally illegal

Illegal under US federal law and the laws of 45+ states, with massive increases in enforcement. Universally criminal across the EU, UK, and virtually all jurisdictions. Internal CSAM prevention guidelines are essential for compliance.

Fully synthetic content (fictional characters) is a legal gray area

In most US states, fully synthetic AI adult content depicting no real person is not explicitly illegal but may be subject to general obscenity laws. However, this space is evolving rapidly — in 2026, legislators are expected to broaden laws to cover AI platforms, payment processors, and hosting providers. The Compliance Associate should monitor this actively.

Platform compliance obligations in Green countries

Even in permissive jurisdictions, AI adult content platforms face growing requirements around age verification (UK, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, multiple US states), content labeling (EU AI Act transparency obligations), takedown procedures (TAKE IT DOWN Act — 48-hour removal requirement by May 2026), and record-keeping.

  2. For Red tier countries, provide a full written risk disclosure and obtain written acknowledgment from the candidate before progressing to offer stage. We strongly recommend that candidates in these jurisdictions seek independent legal advice at their own discretion.
  3. For Yellow tier countries, tailor the disclosure to the role. Content-facing roles warrant a full written disclosure. Non-content roles warrant at minimum a verbal briefing with written acknowledgment.
  4. Prioritize candidates from Green tier countries when possible. These jurisdictions have established legal frameworks for the adult content industry, which reduces friction for both the candidate and the company.
  5. Monitor Sweden, Norway, and Finland closely. Sweden's 2025 law set a precedent that others may follow; the "facilitation" language is broad enough to potentially affect platform employees. Flag this proactively to candidates from these countries.
  6. Engage our Compliance Associate early if you're unsure about a specific jurisdiction or role combination. They can provide additional guidance.
  7. Keep records of all risk disclosures and candidate acknowledgments. This protects the candidate, the agency, and our company.

Candidate Quality: Red Flags & "No-Go" Signals

ℹ️

This guide is for agencies and interviewers conducting early-stage screening. Red flags don't automatically disqualify a candidate — they're signals to explore. Where noted, always ask before drawing conclusions. If a red flag is confirmed, include it in your notes in Ashby.

🚩Lack of enthusiasm and passion for the role, company and product 🚩Someone who didn't prepare for the interview and doesn't ask any questions

A lack of energy or excitement shows disinterest in the company and role, which are critical for success in our fast-paced, innovative environment. Candidates who don't prepare for interviews or fail to ask questions signal a lack of engagement.

⚠️ Explore: What excites them about this role? Did they research the company? What questions do they have for us?

🚩A series of short tenure experiences

Multiple experiences of less than 1.5-2 years may suggest instability or difficulty in long-term commitment. However, given the global layoffs, it's important to ask and understand the reasons behind these short tenures. Make sure to explore whether external factors, such as layoffs, influenced their job changes before making assumptions.

✅ Be fair: Given global layoffs and market shifts, always ask directly: What led to each move? Were exits voluntary, performance-related, or market-driven?

🚩Inability to admit to and learn from past mistakes and failures

Candidates who are unable to acknowledge their failures, or who demonstrate a fear of taking risks, may hinder their personal development and the overall progress of the company.

⚠️ Ask: Tell me about a decision that didn't work out - what did you learn? What would you do differently?

🚩Fear of challenges, not able to work in fast paced environment

It's important for us to have team members who thrive in fast-paced, high-pressure environments and can adapt quickly to change.

⚠️ Watch for: Excessive need for structure, fear of ownership, or avoidance of fast-moving roles.

🚩Discomfort with NSFW content

Discomfort with this type of content would be a significant red flag, as it indicates a mismatch with the company's values and product focus.

✅ Always ask: Have you considered what it means to work with sexually explicit content daily? Have you considered how this might affect you?

🚩Someone who complains a lot about previous experiences or has an overall negative attitude

We aim to foster a positive and collaborative culture. Candidates who frequently complain or focus on negative past experiences are unlikely to contribute to this environment.

⚠️ Dig deeper: Can they articulate what they did enjoy? What they learned, even in tough roles?

🚩Lack of interest in AI

As an AI-focused company, an interest in artificial intelligence is essential. However, it should not be the only motivator.

✅ Ask: What excites you about AI? How do you see its impact on our industry?

🚩Focus on salary and benefits over the role itself 🚩Unrealistic expectations or mismatch of expectations

While compensation matters, we're looking for candidates who are passionate about the role and mission. Those focused primarily on salary or with unrealistic expectations may not align with our company's long-term vision.

⚠️ Look for: Unrealistic expectations, misalignment regarding the role, negotiation-before-fit, or questions solely about perks and working hours.

🚩Someone who shows disrespect, rudeness, or a lack of empathy

A positive, solution-oriented mindset is crucial to fostering a collaborative work environment. Candidates who consistently complain or focus on negatives are unlikely to contribute positively to our culture.

❌ Immediate concerns: Rudeness to recruiters/agencies/coordinators, dismissive comments, or an unwillingness to receive feedback.
