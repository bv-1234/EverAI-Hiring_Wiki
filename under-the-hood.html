Scaling AI companionship — under the hood of a 10B request monolith

💎 Why We Use Rails

Garry Tan recently noted that the world is "sleeping" on the combination of Ruby on Rails and AI-augmented coding. Because Rails is built on "Convention Over Configuration," AI models like Claude can act autonomously within the codebase, resulting in massive productivity gains.

At EverAI, we've turned that "unlock" into a reality at massive scale. We aren't just building an app; we are running a global infrastructure that handles:

28MMonthly Active Users
10B+HTTP requests / 30 days
1 PBMonthly data traffic

📊 The raw numbers

10–12 billion HTTP requests in a typical 30-day window. Peaks push well north of 400M/day. 80M+ visits, 41M+ uniques, and we're serving a full petabyte of data (mostly images/videos — they're heavy).

⚒️ Keeping the queues happy

Everything non-trivial is async: image, messages, notifications, ML inference queues, analytics events etc.. All flowing through Sidekiq.

We process billions of jobs with basically zero backlog. On Rails.

💥 The database is... not small

Single primary Postgres (plus read replicas) holding billions of messages, conversation, user profiles and more.

Total size ~1.81TB and growing fast. Indexing and query optimization never sleep.

We ship fast 🚀

Reviews typically land in under an hour. Engineers spend the vast majority of their time on new features, not firefighting.

Heavy AI tool adoption in the codebase itself. PR velocity has been climbing steadily, with recent months routinely hitting 400–500+ merged PRs.

Join Our Team

We're still early in the journey to 500M+ users. The fun problems ahead: real-time WebSocket scaling for millions of concurrent chats, smarter caching of personalized conversation context, cost-efficient image/video generation at extreme volume, thoughtful monolith sharding, and making the whole system bulletproof under viral traffic spikes.

The core stack is Ruby on Rails + Postgres + Redis + Sidekiq, but we're language-agnostic, strong modern backend/web experience matters way more than specific Rails years.

If any of this sounds like the kind of scale you want to tackle every day, we're hiring senior backend engineers (full remote, Europe time zones preferred).
