📋 Evaluation Framework

1. Technical Depth — 40%

Strong backend engineering experience with real production systems. Can clearly explain their technical contributions, decisions, and the challenges they personally solved — not just what the team did. Familiar with scaling, performance optimisation, and architecture trade-offs. Profile 1: Ruby on Rails, 5+ years preferred for Senior. For frontend-touching roles: familiarity with Turbo, Stimulus, Hotwire is a plus.

Hard Filters: Only worked on small/internal apps with no scaling exposure; vague about their personal technical contributions; can't explain decisions they made; no production experience.

2. Ownership & Collaboration — 25%

Takes initiative, leads technical decisions, and solves problems without waiting for approval. Has worked in teams of 15+ engineers and collaborated cross-functionally with PMs, Designers, QA, and AI/DevOps.

Hard Filters: Always solo or tiny teams only; no signs of ownership or independent decision-making; no cross-functional experience.

3. Mindset & Culture Fit — 20%

Comfortable with startup speed, ambiguity, and pressure. Motivated by impact and ownership — not just salary. Straight-to-the-point communicator in English. Humble and receptive to feedback.

Hard Filters: Corporate/bureaucratic background with no flexibility; low energy or no signs of passion for building; poor English communication; arrogant or dismissive of feedback.

4. Growth & Learning — 15%

Stays current with RoR trends and the broader engineering ecosystem. Uses AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Cursor, etc.) in their daily workflow. Demonstrates self-driven learning — not just passive experience accumulation.

🆕

AI usage is a hard filter
Candidates not using AI at all — professionally or personally — are an immediate no. Those not using it professionally yet can still be considered, depending on their reasoning.

Hard Filters: Not up to date with tech trends; no learning habits or initiative; against using AI tools in development work.
