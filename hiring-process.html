🔀 Hiring Process

1️⃣ Step 1 — Submission & CV Review

Owner: Agency → HM or TA

All candidates must be submitted via Ashby. When adding a candidate, you must @mention Bárbara in the notes and include the following template:

  • Location / Timezone
  • Visa / Right to Work
  • LinkedIn Profile
  • Portfolio or GitHub
  • Salary Expectations
  • Preferred Payment Currency
  • Contract Type
  • Availability
  • Notice Period
  • Other Active Processes
  • Interview Recording Link
  • Notes / Summary

➡️ Once submitted, HM or TA will review the CV, recording, and notes in Ashby.

2️⃣ Step 2 — Initial Screening

Owner: Agency or TA (role dependent)

ℹ️

The screening path depends on the role. Your TA contact will confirm which applies.

Path A — Recording required
For designated roles, the agency conducts the initial screen and uploads an interview recording link directly to the Ashby profile.

Path B — Internal TA screens
For all other roles, our internal TA team picks up the screening once the CV is approved. No action needed from the agency.

⚠️

Our long-term goal is for the in-house team to conduct all initial screenings. However, we do not currently have the capacity for us currently, which is why we have these two paths. Thanks for bearing with us! 🙏

3️⃣ Step 3 — Hiring Manager Interview

Owner: HM

The first live interview with the role owner. Typically 30 minutes.

The discussion focuses on:

  • Relevant experience
  • Problem-solving approach
  • Ownership and execution ability
  • Alignment with the role scope

4️⃣ Step 4 — Case Study / Technical Assessment

Owner: HM or team member (role dependent)

Used to assess real-world working ability, quality of thinking, and how the candidate communicates and structures their work.

Format:

  1. Candidate receives an assignment with a deadline*
    1. *Software Engineering roles: 2-hour live coding challenge instead
    2. *Typically 1 week, but it can be shorter for some roles
  2. HM reviews the submission
  3. A follow-up debrief may take place

5️⃣ Step 5 — Cultural Fit Interview

Owner: Leadership (typically CMO or CTO)

A senior-level conversation focused on:

  • Alignment with our working style
  • Decision-making and ownership mindset
  • Communication and collaboration

6️⃣ Step 6 — Reference Checks

Owner: HM or TA

Before moving to offer, we request 3–4 professional references — ideally former managers, senior colleagues and/or direct reports who worked closely with the candidate.

7️⃣ Step 7 — Offer

Owner: TA

Once all stages are complete:

  1. Compensation and contract terms are finalized internally
  2. Offer is presented to the candidate
  3. Contract and onboarding are handled through Deel, by our HR team

✋ Before You Submit Candidates

Before presenting EverAI to a candidate, make sure they understand what the company does. This is not a standard tech company. Candidates who aren't genuinely excited — or who are uncomfortable with the product — will not pass our process.

Please share the Candidate Handbook with every candidate before submission.

🔴 The NSFW Conversation

⚠️

This must happen before every submission, without exception. Do not submit a candidate who has not been explicitly asked about this.

EverAI's products involve sexually explicit AI content. Candidates will work or be exposed to NSFW material daily / weekly, depending on their role — whether in engineering, marketing, operations, or content roles. This is not incidental; it is central to the product.

We are fully transparent about this from the very beginning of the process. We never hide it, and we are always upfront about it with every candidate.

How to raise it:

"EverAI operates in the adult AI companionship space. This means working with explicit content. Have you thought about what that looks like in practice, and are you fully comfortable with it?"

If a candidate hesitates, it's usually better not to pursue and not to submit. ❌
