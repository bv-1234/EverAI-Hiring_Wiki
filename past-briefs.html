AI LLM Tech Lead

🗄️

🧑‍🧑‍🧒‍🧒 Team & Environment Context

  • This is not a research role and not a pure people-management role. We want a builder who has done the hard technical work themselves — and can also lead a small team of AI engineers from the front.
  • The mission: own the "brain" and architecture of our core product at EverAI / Candy.ai — the conversational AI experience that is central to everything we do.
  • The Lead will not have time to learn hard skills on the job. They need to arrive with hands-on fine-tuning experience already behind them.
  • The Content: Primarily Candy.ai and YumeAI.com. Candidates must be 100% comfortable working with NSFW/explicit training data and building moderation for it. NSFW background is a plus, not a requirement.
  • Remote, fast-paced scale-up environment. No bureaucracy, no long feedback loops.
⚠️

Critical Context: The most important thing is not the scale of tokens or infrastructure — it's whether the AI/chatbot product they worked on was core to the business. A candidate who built a central, user-facing conversational product at a company of 500K+ users is more relevant than someone who optimized inference at a large company where AI was a side feature.

🎯 Calibration Anchors & Target Profiles

We are looking for candidates where the conversational AI or chatbot was the product — not a feature.

  • Consumer AI & Companion Platforms
  • Creative & Roleplay AI
  • High-Scale Conversational Products
  • LLM-first startups where the chat experience is the core value proposition

Minimum size guidance: the AI should have been the central revenue-generating and/or impactful feature of the business (it does not matter if it's B2B or B2C).

⛔️ Non-Negotiables

  1. Hands-on fine-tuning experience — must have done it themselves, for external user-facing products (not internal tooling). Key question: what did you fine-tune, and why? Fine-tuning must represent ~60%+ of their AI experience (agentic AI / RAG / prompt engineering alone is not a fit). Post-training experience is mandatory; pre-training is a nice-to-have.
  2. The AI product was core / critical to the business — not a side project, not an internal tool. It's about ownership, impact and risk. Scope includes LLMs and Computer Vision — both are valid.
  3. Leadership experience is a must — being senior is not enough. Minimum 1–2 years of hands-on leadership experience required. Must still be technically hands-on.
  4. Strong English communication skills — this role requires clear technical and cross-functional communication.
  5. NSFW comfort — must be comfortable with explicit training data and content moderation in this context.
ℹ️

Good Keywords to Look out for

Models & frameworks — core: Mistral · Llama · DeepSeek · Qwen · Hugging Face

Production & inference — strong signal: vLLM · PyTorch · inference optimization · open-source LLM

Fine-tuning techniques — high signal: SFT · RLHF · DPO · LoRA · QLoRA · PEFT

Fine-tuning frameworks — niche, very high signal: Axolotl · Unsloth · LitGPT

📋 Evaluation Framework

1. Fine-Tuning & Model Alignment — 40%

Has personally fine-tuned models for external, user-facing products. Understands SFT, RLHF, and DPO not just in theory but from direct experience. Can speak concretely to what they fine-tuned, why, and what the outcome was. Familiar with data engineering — curating, cleaning, and synthetic data generation.

Hard Filters: Fine-tuning experience limited to internal tooling or demos; cannot describe a specific fine-tuning project and its business impact; prompt engineering only.

2. Technical Leadership — 25%

Player/coach profile. Sets technical direction and owns the architecture while remaining hands-on in the codebase. Has led small teams of senior engineers, conducted code reviews, and maintained a technical roadmap. Does not delegate everything — writes code.

Hard Filters: Pure managers who no longer code; pure ICs with no leadership experience.

3. Production Engineering — 25%

Has shipped conversational AI to real users at scale. Comfortable with the modern LLM stack: PyTorch, Hugging Face, vLLM or equivalent inference engines. Understands latency, throughput, and cost trade-offs in production — not just in benchmarks.

Hard Filters: Academic or research-only profiles; no production deployment experience.

4. Content Moderation Architecture — 10%

Understands how to build nuanced, context-aware moderation — not binary safe/unsafe filters. Knows the difference between lazy safety filtering and intelligent classifier-based moderation. NSFW experience is a plus, not a hard requirement.

Hard Filters: Strong ideological objection to working with NSFW content or explicit training data.

⁉️ FAQs

Technical

1. Model Strategy & Ownership

Are you building proprietary models, or fine-tuning open-source / API models? We fine-tune open-source models and also use some APIs. The majority of our traffic — roughly 80–90% — runs on self-hosted fine-tuned open-source models.

What % of usage is OpenAI / Anthropic APIs vs self-hosted? 10–20% third-party API, 80–90% self-hosted fine-tuned models.

What is your long-term model strategy? Our strategy is to stay on top of the frontier of open-source models and fine-tune them to match our user preferences. We've tested very large open-source models, and our smaller in-house fine-tuned model still outperforms them on our roleplay evaluation metrics. Roleplay is a highly specific use case, and we believe owning the fine-tuning loop is a durable edge.

How differentiated is your model vs competitors? On our internal roleplay evals, our fine-tuned smaller model outperforms significantly larger out-of-the-box open-source and closed-source models. We haven't run a formal head-to-head benchmark against competitor products, but our qualitative signal — user retention, session length, and like rates — suggests a meaningful quality gap in our favor for this specific use case.

2. Infrastructure & Scale

What is the infra stack (cloud, orchestration, serving)? Everything runs on GKE, managed by a 5-person DevOps team.

How are you handling your token volume technically? We serve multi-billion tokens/day of combined input/output on GKE, running across a mix of GPU types.

Latency benchmarks? Kept private. We target sub-2 second P50 latency on self-hosted models for the chat experience.

Cost per request? Kept private, but cost-efficiency is a core optimization axis given our scale.

GPU setup / budget? Budget kept private. Stack runs on modern datacenter GPUs on GKE, e.g. H100s/A100s, RTX PRO.

Any bottlenecks today (infra vs model vs product)? On the infra side, the main challenge is serving larger models (32B+) at efficient speed and cost. But the bigger challenge is on the modeling side — training a state-of-the-art roleplay model. That's precisely where this hire comes in.

3. Data & Training Pipelines

What is the source of training data? A mix of sources including curated internal data and synthetic generation. Specific breakdowns are kept private, but the candidate would have meaningful data to work with from day one.

User conversations? Synthetic generation? Yes, synthetic generation is part of the mix. Details on both kept private.

How do you handle data labeling? We have an internal process in place. Specifics kept private, but scaling and improving the labeling pipeline is one of the areas we expect this hire to help evaluate.

Preference data for RLHF/DPO? Yes, we run preference-based post-training. The maturity of this pipeline and how to scale it is one of the concrete areas the new hire would help shape.

What tooling exists for experiment tracking / evaluation? We have tooling in place for both. Specifics kept private, but improving experimentation velocity and evaluation maturity are part of the scope.

How mature is your eval framework (offline + online)? Offline: solid and roleplay-specific. Online: earlier stage — this is explicitly part of the 90-day scope.

4. Moderation & Safety

How does EverGuard actually work? It's a hybrid approach combining model-based classifiers and rules. Specific architecture details are kept private.

What % of engineering effort is moderation vs core product? Around 25% moderation, 75% core product.

What are the hardest unsolved moderation problems today? The hardest ongoing challenges are around context-dependent nuance — staying permissive enough for legitimate creative/roleplay use cases while catching genuinely harmful patterns, and keeping false-positive rates low so the experience doesn't feel over-censored. These are active areas of work rather than blockers.

Any regulatory exposure? (EU AI Act risk, etc.) No material exposure at this time. We monitor the regulatory landscape proactively.

5. Architecture & Engineering Reality

What does the current system architecture look like end-to-end? Specifics kept private, but the stack is GKE-based with self-hosted inference, a fine-tuning pipeline, moderation layer, and the product surface.

Where is the biggest technical debt? Our training pipeline could be more automated and easier to iterate on — today, running a new fine-tuning cycle takes more manual orchestration than we'd like. Our online eval infrastructure is also less mature than our offline side. Both are explicitly part of the scope for this hire.

How mature is CI/CD? Monitoring? Incident handling? CI/CD and monitoring are functional but have room to improve — solid foundation on core infra, less mature on model-level observability. Incident handling is strong, with clear on-call rotations and a good uptime track record.

How often are models deployed to production? Every 2–3 months depending on cycles and team focus. A faster release cadence is something we want to move toward.

6. Product Performance & Metrics

What are the core success metrics for AI quality? Retention, revenue per user, session length, and like rates.

What are current failure modes in user experience? The biggest one is personalization — we serve a user base with very different preferences (short messages vs long narrative, different content styles, different roleplay patterns) and our current model doesn't yet adapt dynamically enough to each user. There are also the usual roleplay-model failure modes: occasional consistency drift over long sessions, and edge cases where the model doesn't quite hit the desired tone.

Where is the biggest gap — Intelligence / Latency / Safety? Primarily intelligence and personalization. Latency and safety are in a good place today, though we keep investing in both.

7. Immediate Scope for This Hire

What are the top 3 problems this person must solve in 90 days? (1) Deeply understand our current training pipeline, identify bottlenecks, and propose concrete improvements. (2) Work closely with the engineering team to upgrade our fine-tuning pipeline, and partner with the CTO and leadership to define the success metrics and KPIs that define what "a better model" actually means for us. (3) Bring product-minded ideas for how to push the roleplay chat experience to the next level, in close collaboration with the product team. By the end of 90 days, the expectation is a clear plan for what a better model looks like, plus concrete initiatives to improve the in-chat user experience.

What is currently blocking growth that this hire unlocks? Two things. First, it frees up significant CTO bandwidth. Second, and more importantly, it brings dedicated ML leadership into the room. Our CTO's background is in web engineering, and we want a peer who owns the ML depth — someone who can partner with him on technical direction and push our AI engineers on the modeling decisions that will define the next phase of the product.

How much freedom vs alignment with CTO? Trust is earned, and ownership is granted as soon as trust is established. We value people who take real ownership and move fast.

Anyone who shows extreme dedication, motivation, and business impact can grow into larger responsibilities. The initial framing is senior IC / player-coach with a clear path upward.

5. Culture Reality Check

6. Risk & Controversy

We are looking for Video Editors who specialize in paid social ads — specifically Meta and TikTok. This is not a generalist editing role and not a cinematic/narrative role. Every edit must be built to convert: hook in the first 2 seconds, fast iteration, data-driven decisions.

Daily work involves NSFW and explicit creatives for Candy.ai and YumeAI. Do not submit candidates unless NSFW comfort has been explicitly confirmed on a call.

🧑‍🧑‍🧒‍🧒 Team & Environment Context

  • The Team: Reports directly to the CMO, working alongside 3 Creatives. The candidate can be Mid or Senior.
  • High-Velocity Pipeline: We move at the speed of AI. They must manage their own pipeline independently — from idea inception to final color grading and sound design.
  • The Content: Candy.ai and YumeAI. We operate in the AI companionship space. 100% comfort with NSFW/explicit creatives is a non-negotiable baseline requirement.
  • Data-Obsessed Iteration: We iterate based on KPIs (CTR, CR). If an ad doesn't perform, we don't fix it — we pivot and cut a new version immediately.
  • AI-Mandatory Workflow: AI is core to how we work, not a bonus skill. Candidates must be prompt engineers who use Midjourney, Runway, KlingAI, or equivalent tools to create assets that don't yet exist.

🎯 Calibration Anchors & Target Profiles

Ideal backgrounds:

  • High-volume DTC agencies, Gaming, iGaming, or Dating/Social app marketing
  • Company examples: Tango Me, Kilo Health, V-Shred, TubeScience, etc.
  • Bonus: prior experience in Adult, Dating, or iGaming industries

⛔️ Non-Negotiables

  1. NSFW comfort — 100% dealbreaker; must be explicitly confirmed before submission
  2. Paid social portfolio — majority must be Meta and/or TikTok ads; no cinematic-only portfolios
  3. Performance mindset — understands Hook-Body-CTA; can cut multiple hook variations for the same ad; knows what a retention graph is and how to act on it
  4. Full technical autonomy — handles sound design, colour grading, and motion graphics independently; does not require a separate sound engineer, colorist, or motion designer to finish a 30-second ad
  5. AI fluency — uses generative AI tools (Midjourney, Runway, KlingAI, or equivalent) in daily professional workflow; not resistant to AI; sees it as a primary tool
  6. Speed — first draft within hours; multiple iterations per week
  7. Fluent English — can communicate clearly about creative decisions and performance rationale

🛠️ Tools

Essential: Premiere Pro and/or Davinci Resolve and/or Final Cut and/or Avid Media Composer.
Very important: After Effects, Photoshop.
Great to have: Illustrator, Figma, Eleven Labs, Topaz.
Amazing to have: ComfyUI.
AI tools/platforms great to have proficiency in: Kling AI, VEO, Sora, Nano Banana, Midjourney, Runway, Qwen, Higgsfield, Heygen.

📋 Evaluation Framework

1. Performance-Driven Storytelling — 35%

Deep understanding of the Hook-Body-CTA framework. Can manipulate pacing, motion, and sound to drive user action. Able to look at a retention graph and identify exactly where users drop off. Comfortable cutting multiple hook variations for the same ad to find the winning angle. Can optimise for multiple formats (9:16, 1:1, 16:9) and channel-specific best practices (TikTok style vs. adult pre-roll).

Hard Filters: Cinematic-only portfolios. If the first 2 seconds of their work don't grab attention — reject.

2. Generative AI & Tool Mastery — 30%

Proven experience using generative AI tools (Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, Runway, Luma, KlingAI, etc.) in a professional workflow. Uses AI for advanced tasks: generating custom B-roll, face-swapping, generative fill, AI-voice integration. Deep expertise in Adobe Premiere Pro, After Effects, or equivalent.

Hard Filters: Resistance to AI tools or a traditional-only mindset. If they see AI as a threat rather than a primary tool, they won't fit.

3. Technical Autonomy & Sound Design — 20%

Fully independent in the editing process — including sound design, color grading, and motion graphics in After Effects. Manages their own render queues, file structures, and technical exports for high-volume, daily iteration.

Hard Filters: Requires a separate sound engineer, colorist, or motion designer to finish a 30-second ad.

4. Startup Grit & Communication — 15%

Perfectly fluent English. Can explain why an edit will convert a specific target audience. Thrives in fast, messy environments. Willing to kill an edit they spent hours on and start over if the data says to. Prioritizes speed and performance over artistic prestige.

Hard Filters: High sensitivity to NSFW content; needs more than 48 hours for a first draft; job-hoppers under 1 year; no concrete recent win stories.
